Hey visitor! I see you’ve happened across my personal site, so it would be fair for me to introduce myself. My name is Matt and I am a web developer currently based in NJ.

This wasn’t always the case, however. I knew for the longest time, I always had an interest in people and how they work together in the different places in society. I previously had attended the University of Delaware for Business Administration, only to find that a business degree wasn’t in the stars. But that’s ok, because it gave me an opportunity to pursue and receive an Associates in Sociology from my local community college. But life can be funny sometimes, you know? All this career searching had lead me to two realizations.

-Sociology had become an integral part of my life, changing how I viewed many aspects of society and how I interfaced with them.
-Being an avid video gamer and constant internet user through the years practically lead me to a new development (ha) of my life that became my current journey through web development.

So here I am. My name is Matt, I love to talk about society and the relationships of people in communities. I also discovered that web development is an extremely rewarding experience and also allows me to express my love of sociology in a world of connected people using cutting edge technology.

Vibe

A social application built with Ruby on Rails framework and utilizes a PostgreSQL database. Allows the user to map their current mood as a colored rating to the Google Map API, alongside other users’ posts to visualize moods in a certain geographic location.

Armchair Philosophy

A lightweight blogging application built using Backbone.js and the MEAN stack. Designed for users to log thoughts with intent to discuss daily philosophies with other users. The main profile page lists other usernames and all related posts to those usernames.

Proficient Provisions

A food service application using Sinatra and a PostgreSQL database. To be used by wait staff, the user can record and track orders placed by the party. Once an order is placed, the proper table on the grid will turn red to denote a filled table.

Musical Timeline

A musically themed web experience which allows users to create profiles and reflect on popular American music from previous decades as they listen. Built in Rails using the Spotify Web Player with Backbone.js.
